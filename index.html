Course Description

The graphics course is taught over 6 weeks and features a project with both a presentation and submission component.

Class Project

By the conclusion of this course, you will be expected to write a short survey paper on a graphics topic of your choice. The paper should be 4-8 pages long including figures and will be supplemented by a live demo & 7-10 minute presentation completed in class during the final week of the period. You may choose from one of the following topics below for your paper:

  • Bresenham's Algorithm(s)
  • ACES Color Mapping
  • Billboarding in 3D environments
  • Cell Shading & Non-realistic effects
  • Ray Marching (See ShaderToy for examples)
  • Physics Simulation (Navier Stokes)
  • Hierarchical Acceleration Data Structures (Octrees, BVH, etc)
  • Binary Space Partitioning (BSP) Trees and their applications
  • Potentially Visible Sets (PVS) and their applications
  • Hair & Fur Simulation
  • Caustics & Refractive Media in Shading or Light Transport
  • Geometry or Tesselation Shaders
  • G-Buffer or Visibility Buffers
  • An approved topic of your choice

Course Overview:

0. Introduction

  • Representations and Methods (Discrete, Parametric, etc & Raster, Trace, Radiosity)
  • Rasteriztion Pipeline Overview

1. Points, Lines, & Curves

  • Bresenham, Wu Algorithms
  • Bezier Curves
  • OpenGL Immediate Mode

  • ITW: Create_Matrix, Draw_Line, Draw_Pixel, Set_Color
  • GL: glClearColor, glBegin, glVertex2f
  • PROJECT: Points, Lines, & Curves
  • TECHNICAL: Vertex Shader, Homogeneous Coordinates, Canvas Rendering

2. Transformations, Barycentric Coordinates & Sampling

  • Scanline and parallel rasterization of triangles
  • Sampling Techniques for Triangles and Images
  • OpenGL Retained Mode
  • ITW: draw_triangle, display_lists, create_vbo, create_vbo, bind_vbo, bind_vao, set_shader(VERTEX & FRAGMENT, ...)
  • GL: glGenBuffers, glDeleteBuffers, glVertexAttrib, glBindVertexArray, glBindBuffer
  • PROJECT: Smooth Triangles & Texturing, Billboarded Planets In Motion (2D)
  • TECHNICAL: Fragment Shader, MSAA, FSAA, Fragment vs Pixel, Z & Stencil Buffers, Math Products

3. Transformations, Projection and Multi-Pass Rendering

  • Rotation, Translation, Scaling, Homogeneous Coordinates
  • Matrix multiplication, inversion, concatenation of matrices
  • Object Hierarchy & Scene Graph
  • Fractals: Julia, Mandelbrot, IFS
  • Viewport Transformations
  • Perspective (Similar Triangles), Orthographic Matrices
  • Clipping algorithms
  • Perspective Correction
  • Model, View, Projection matrices
  • Multi Pass Rendering

  • ITW: create_renderpass, bind_render_pass, set_uniform, create_uniform, bind_uniform, clipping
  • GL: glBindRenderTarget, glUniform
  • PROJECT: Shadow Mapped 3D View
  • TECHNICAL: Projection, Clipping, PCF, Multi-Pass Rendering, Depth & Stencil Buffers

4. Compute & Curves & Surfaces

  • Bezier Curves (Cameras and GPU Textured projection)
  • Compute Based Catmull Clark Surfaces
  • Transform Feedback, Geometry, Tesselation, and Compute Shaders
  • Patches and Nerbs
  • Texture Based SDFs, Blinn Cubic Bezier Renderer

  • ITW: create_renderpass, bind_render_pass
  • GL: glUniform, glDispatchCompute
  • PROJECT: Glyph Renderer & 2D Camera Motion
  • TECHNICAL: Geometry, Tesselation, Compute Shaders, Bezier, Scanline Rendering, Fonts

5. Shading & Light Models

  • Normal Transformations
  • Dot, Cross Products, Wedge Products
  • Point, Directiona, Area Lights
  • Ambient, Diffuse, Phong Shading
  • Post Processing, HDR, Gamma Correction
  • Cubemaps, Skyboxes, and IBR
