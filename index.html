Course Description

The graphics course is taught over 6 weeks and features a project with both a presentation and submission component.

Course Overview:

0. Introduction

  • Representations and Methods (Discrete, Parametric, etc & Raster, Trace, Radiosity)
  • Rasteriztion Pipeline Overview

Class Project

By the conclusion of this course, you will be expected to write a short survey paper on a graphics topic of your choice. The paper should be 4-8 pages long including figures and will be supplemented by a live demo & 7-10 minute presentation completed in class during the final week of the period. You may choose from one of the following topics below for your paper:

  • Bresenham's Algorithm(s)
  • ACES Color Mapping
  • Billboarding in 3D environments
  • Extensions to Cel Shading & Other Non-realistic effects
  • Ray Marching (See ShaderToy for examples)
  • Physics Simulation (Navier Stokes)
  • Hierarchical Acceleration Data Structures (Octrees, BVH, etc)
  • Binary Space Partitioning (BSP) Trees and their applications
  • Potentially Visible Sets (PVS) and their applications
  • Hair & Fur Simulation
  • Caustics & Refractive Media in Shading or Light Transport
  • Geometry or Tesselation Shaders
  • G-Buffer or Visibility Buffers
  • An approved topic of your choice
